\section{Model}\whenintegrated{\label{model}}
\whenintegrated{\label{sec:model}} % integrated doc: SST for labels

This section describes the economic environment used in the model.
Households receive stochastic income and choose consumption and savings
subject to a budget constraint.

\subsection{Households}

Households maximize expected lifetime utility.
As a simple example, consumption may consist of two parts:

\begin{equation}
c_{t} = c_{sp,t} + c_{opt,t}
\end{equation}

where $c_{sp,t}$ denotes ``splurge'' consumption and $c_{opt,t}$ is the
optimized component of consumption.

\subsection{Income Process}

Households receive stochastic labor income that depends on employment
status and income shocks. The detailed specification follows the model
described in the original paper.

\subsection{Recessions and Policies}

The model allows for recession shocks and several fiscal policy
responses, such as stimulus checks, unemployment benefit extensions,
and payroll tax cuts.

\subsection{Aggregate Demand Effects}

An extension of the model introduces feedback from aggregate
consumption to income through an aggregate demand channel.
